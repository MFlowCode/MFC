# JWL EOS Notes

This page documents the Jones-Wilkins-Lee (JWL) equation-of-state support used by the five-equation model in MFC.

## Pure JWL Products

For products density `rho`, specific internal energy `e`, and relative volume `V = rho0/rho`, MFC evaluates

```text
p = A (1 - omega/(R1 V)) exp(-R1 V)
  + B (1 - omega/(R2 V)) exp(-R2 V)
  + omega rho e.
```

The cold-curve pressure is evaluated through a shared helper so pressure recovery, energy recovery, and sound-speed code use the same expression.

## Mixture Closures

The JWL mixture closure applies to five-equation mixtures with one JWL products fluid and one non-JWL ambient fluid, which may be an ideal gas (air) or a (mild) stiffened gas (see "Stiffened-Gas Ambient" below). Whenever a JWL fluid is present, MFC applies a composition (heat-capacity) weighted single-fluid closure. Let the products' heat-capacity share be `w = Y*cv_products/(Y*cv_products + (1 - Y)*cv_air)`; then the JWL amplitudes are `A_n = w*A`, `B_n = w*B`, the Grüneisen coefficient is `omega = Gamma_air + w*(omega0 - Gamma_air)`, and the heat capacity is the mass-weighted `cv = Y*cv_products + (1 - Y)*cv_air`. Because every coefficient depends only on `Y` (independent of `rho` and `e`), the analytic pressure-to-energy inverse and the closed-form sound speed stay exact, and the closure is exact at `Y = 0` (ambient law) and `Y = 1` (pure JWL) with no handover smoothstep required. Weighting by composition rather than density keeps `omega` relaxing toward `omega0` as products fill the cell — the density-ramp Rocflu closure (`modflu/RFLU_ModJWL.F90`) never reached `rho0` in afterburn mixing and overshot `p`/`c`; the weighted form matches full pressure-temperature equilibrium to ~1e-8 in the weak-pressure regime.

This closure is not TNT-specific. MFC reads `jwl_A`, `jwl_B`, `jwl_R1`, `jwl_R2`, `jwl_omega`, `jwl_rho0`, `cv`, and either `jwl_Q` or `jwl_E0` from the JWL fluid, plus `jwl_air_rho0` and either `jwl_air_e0` or `jwl_air_p0` for the co-existing gas, so any explosive products model with a valid JWL parameter set can be used. `jwl_Q` is the specific detonation energy in J/kg; internally MFC derives `jwl_E0 = jwl_rho0*jwl_Q`. If both are provided, they must be consistent. The ambient-gas Grüneisen coefficient is taken from the ideal-gas fluid's own `gamma` (`Gamma_air = 1/gamma`; with a single JWL fluid the JWL fluid's own `gamma` is used), and the optional `jwl_ej_rho_ref` sets the products-energy reference density (default `jwl_rho0`). Unlike Rocflupicl's case-specific implementation, MFC does not hard-code TNT density, TNT energy scaling, ambient density, ambient energy, or air heat capacity inside the EOS. The reference parameters must satisfy `jwl_rho0 > jwl_air_rho0` and `jwl_E0/jwl_ej_rho_ref > jwl_air_e0`.

For convenience, `toolchain/mfc/jwl_products.py` ships validated parameter sets for common explosives (`TNT`, `LX-10`, `PETN`, `Composition B`, `HMX`, `PBX-9404`); a case can build its JWL fluid with `from mfc.jwl_products import TNT, AIR, jwl_fluid, ambient_fluid` rather than transcribing coefficients (see `examples/3D_jwl_reactive_air_burst/case.py`). An inconsistent set still fails fast at startup via the init closure scan below.

## Stiffened-Gas Ambient (Condensed)

When the non-JWL fluid has `pi_inf > 0`, MFC treats it as a stiffened gas. The same composition weight `w` drives a cold-stiffness offset `pi_hat = (1 - w)*pi_inf` (so the mixture carries `pi_c = (Gamma + 1)*pi_hat`); everything else in the weighted closure is unchanged. The pure-ambient limit (`Y = 0`, `w = 0`) recovers `p = Gamma*rho*e - (Gamma + 1)*pi_inf` exactly, and `pi_inf = 0` reduces bit-identically to the ideal-gas closure. The true stiffness is derived from the ambient fluid's own inputs (`pi_inf_true = fluid_pp%pi_inf/(fluid_pp%gamma + 1)`); no new case parameters are needed, and `jwl_air_p0` resolves the ambient energy through the stiffened form `e0 = (p0*gamma + pi_inf)/rho0` (MFC-convention `gamma`/`pi_inf`). Because `pi_c` is independent of both `e` and `rho`, the analytic pressure-to-energy inverse remains exact and the Grüneisen sound-speed identity is unchanged. The pressure floor doubles as the standard cavitation (pressure-cutoff) model, and the sound-speed safety floor becomes `min(Gamma, omega)*(p + pi_hat)/rho`. Note the closure's temperature remains `T = e/cv` in the pure-ambient limit, which for a stiffened liquid counts the cold (stiffness) energy as thermal; `T` is a diagnostic on this path (pressure and sound speed are `cv`-free) and is not a physical liquid temperature.

**Range of validity — water is not supported.** This is a single-fluid closure: at intermediate `Y` both constituents are evaluated at the shared mixture density. For a mildly stiffened ambient (`pi_inf` well below the mixture pressures) this is accurate, but for a water-scale stiffness (`pi_inf ~ 3.4e8` Pa) the stiff-liquid pressure is so density-sensitive that no single scalar weight can reach pressure-temperature equilibrium — the equilibrium weight is non-universal and unbounded — so products/water mixtures produce unphysical (negative) mixture-cell pressures. Underwater/condensed products+water work therefore requires a genuine multi-fluid closure (a Kuhl/kpw or an N-constituent Mie-Grüneisen pressure-equilibrium model), which is a planned extension hooking in at `s_jwl_rocflu_coeffs`; it is not implemented today. The shipped `Stiffened` closure test uses a deliberately mild (`pi_inf ~ 5e7` Pa, non-water) ambient.

Finite pressure, temperature, and energy floors are applied only after explicit finite checks. The state routine returns the raw squared sound speed; the public wrappers bound it below by `Gamma_air*p/rho`, a safety floor that sits below any physical mixture sound speed (pure air gives `(Gamma_air + 1)*p/rho`, pure products `(omega + 1)*p/rho` plus cold-curve terms) so it engages only on unphysical states. NaNs are otherwise intentionally preserved so bad states are visible during debugging instead of being converted into plausible-looking floor values.

## Reaction Sources

Three optional detonation-energy sources sit on top of the closure (all default off, so a plain products-expansion run is unaffected):

- **Program burn** (`prog_burn`): a Rocflu-style kinematic front expands from the detonation point (`pb_x_det`/`pb_y_det`/`pb_z_det`, time `pb_t_det`) at speed `pb_D_cj`, depositing the detonation energy `jwl_Q` over a reaction zone of width `pb_width`. No extra field is transported; each swept cell receives exactly `jwl_Q` per unit explosive mass. The front must not advance more than one band width per step (`pb_D_cj*dt <= pb_width`, enforced at startup for constant-`dt` runs), and 3D cylindrical grids are not supported (the azimuthal coordinate is an angle, so the Cartesian front distance is ill-defined there; 2D axisymmetric with the charge on the axis is fine).
- **Afterburn** (`jwl_afterburn`): products-air combustion energy `jwl_q_ab` released through an advected progress variable, either mixing-rate (`jwl_ab_model = 1`, time scale `jwl_ab_tau`) or Arrhenius (`jwl_ab_model = 2`, default; `jwl_ab_A`, `jwl_ab_theta`, `jwl_ab_n`). Requires an ideal-gas ambient. Since `jwl_q_ab` adds to `jwl_Q`, use a detonation-only JWL fit to avoid double counting. The rate is clamped so the progress variable cannot overshoot 1 within a step, capping the release at the remaining `Y*jwl_q_ab` budget even for stiff rate parameters (same clamp as the reactive burn).
- **Reactive burn** (`jwl_reactive`, JWL++ after Souers 2000): a self-propagating detonation driven by the local pressure, `dλ/dt = jwl_G·p^jwl_b_exp·(1−λ)`, releasing `jwl_Q` as the explosive reacts. Mutually exclusive with `prog_burn`; `jwl_G`/`jwl_b_exp` are calibrated per explosive to reproduce its CJ velocity.

## Validation Scope

The production closure is covered by registered golden tests: the products-air weighted closure (with a homogeneous 50/50 slab exercising the mixture coefficients at intermediate `Y`), a mild stiffened-gas ambient (exercising the `pi_c` cold-offset path), the program-burn plus afterburn sources, and the JWL++ reactive burn. Beyond the golden regression, verified physics includes: the afterburn and JWL++ energy budgets closing to their `Y·q` bounds (<1%); the program-burn front speed matching `pb_D_cj`; and the 3D free-air TNT blast tracking the Kinney-Graham overpressure curve in the far field (`benchmarks/3D_jwl_spherical_tnt_free_air_validation/validate_kingery.py`).

At initialization MFC scans the closure over the configured material's `(rho, e, Y)` envelope and aborts if any state yields a non-positive or non-finite squared sound speed, or if the pressure-to-energy round-trip fails to recover the input energy. An inconsistent parameter set therefore fails fast at startup rather than producing silently wrong states during the run. The scanned envelope is `rho` in `[0.1*jwl_air_rho0, 4*jwl_rho0]` (capped at `2*jwl_rho0` for a stiffened ambient) and `e` in `[0.5*air_e0, 5*e_j]`; this covers CJ and reflected-shock states for a gaseous ambient, but states beyond the stiffened-ambient cap (e.g. reflected shocks past twice the products reference density under water) remain floor-protected without the init-time verification guarantee.

The closure follows the JWL pressure, temperature, inverse-energy, and sound-speed formulas with composition-weighted coefficients; the amplitudes are constant in `e`, so the pressure-to-energy inverse is a single closed-form expression (no piecewise energy branches), removing the legacy Rocflu fallback's pressure/energy round-trip mismatch.
